## Features

**Section headline:**
Three functions. One accountable layer.

**Section subhead:**
Everything SANEX does answers a single question at the moment it matters: is this action safe and authorized, and who owns it?

**Feature 1 — Verification**
Title: Know before you sign.
Body: An independent, plain-language verdict on every counterparty, contract, and request — delivered the moment you're asked to approve it, in words anyone can act on.

**Feature 2 — Mandate enforcement**
Title: Limits that actually hold.
Body: Set what you and your agents are allowed to do — which assets, how much, where, how often. SANEX enforces those limits from the outside, so nothing can exceed the authority it was given.

**Feature 3 — Attribution and recourse**
Title: A record you can stand behind.
Body: Every action is attributable to an actor and a mandate, and every decision is written to a clear, portable record — for your own review, and for anyone who has to answer for it later.

**Section closing line:**
No new asset. No leverage. Just accountability, applied where decisions are made.

## FAQ

**Section headline:**
Questions, answered plainly.

**Is SANEX a token?**
No. SANEX is infrastructure. If a token ever exists, it does one job — metering usage and governing standards. It is not something to trade.

**Why does it have to be independent?**
Because a safety layer is only believable when it doesn't belong to the party being checked. SANEX answers to the user, not to the venues or agents it oversees.

**Will it slow me down?**
Only before something dangerous or unauthorized. Safe actions pass without friction. The pause exists exactly where a mistake would be irreversible.

**Does it work with autonomous agents?**
Yes. Agent limits are enforced from the outside, so an agent cannot exceed the authority you gave it — and every action it takes is recorded.

**Who holds my funds?**
You do. SANEX is non-custodial. It verifies, enforces, and records; it never takes possession of your assets.

**What do I actually get?**
A clear verdict before you sign, limits that hold, and a record you can stand behind — carried with you across everything you use.
